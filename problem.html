The Global Education Crisis

Across the world, millions of children are unable to attend school or receive a quality education. Poverty, conflict, and lack of resources prevent many children from accessing learning opportunities.

Key Facts

  • UNICEF: 64 million primary school age children remain out of school.
  • UNESCO: Around 251 million children and youth are currently not in school.
  • World Bank: 70% of 10-year-olds in low and middle income countries cannot read a simple text.
  • OECD PISA 2022: 45% of students are low performers in at least one core subject.
  • European Commission: About 30% of students in the EU fail to reach minimum maths proficiency.
  • UN SDG4: Global progress on education access has slowed significantly since 2015.

These numbers highlight a global crisis — but solutions exist. Governments, charities, and communities around the world are working to close education gaps.
