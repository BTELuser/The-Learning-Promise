What is Sustainable Development Goal 4?

The fourth Sustainable Development Goal was established by the United Nations in 2015 as part of the global Sustainable Development Goals framework. Goal 4 focuses on ensuring inclusive and equitable quality education and promoting lifelong learning opportunities for all people around the world.

Education is widely recognised as one of the most powerful tools for reducing poverty, improving health, strengthening economies, and creating equal opportunities for individuals. When people have access to quality education, they are more likely to find stable employment, participate in democratic societies, and contribute positively to their communities.

However, access to education remains unequal across the world. Many children and young people still face barriers to attending school or receiving quality education. Goal 4 aims to address these challenges by improving access to schools, increasing the number of qualified teachers, and ensuring that learning environments are safe and inclusive.

Organisations such as UNESCO, UNICEF, and the World Bank work together with governments to improve education systems, provide learning resources, and support educational development across different regions of the world.

Key Targets of Goal 4

  • Ensure free, equitable, and quality primary and secondary education for all children.
  • Provide equal access to affordable technical, vocational, and higher education.
  • Increase the number of qualified teachers through training and international cooperation.
  • Build and improve education facilities that are safe, inclusive, and effective learning environments.
  • Promote lifelong learning opportunities for people of all ages.
