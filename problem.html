The Problem: Global Education

The Problem: Global Education Crisis

  • ~251 million children & youth are out of school worldwide (UNESCO)
  • In low-income countries, 33% of school-age children are out of school vs 3% in high-income countries (UNESCO)
  • Over 57% of children worldwide are in "learning poverty" — cannot read a simple text by age 10 (World Bank)
  • Education spending gap: $55/student in low-income countries vs $8,500/student in high-income countries (UNESCO)
  • Socio-economic inequality strongly affects PISA 2022 student performance (OECD)

Main Factors Affecting Education in LEDCs

Children living in poverty often lack access to school or learning materials. Malnutrition and poor health also reduce their ability to attend or focus in school.
Low government spending limits classrooms, teachers, and resources, making quality education difficult to provide.
Lack of safe buildings, electricity, clean water, and technology hampers teaching and learning, particularly in rural areas.
Undertrained or underpaid teachers negatively affect instruction quality and students’ learning outcomes.
Malnutrition and diseases decrease school attendance and concentration, reducing the ability to learn.
War, displacement, and instability force children out of school and disrupt education systems entirely.
Socio-economic disparities within countries mean poorer children often have worse learning opportunities, affecting literacy and numeracy outcomes.
